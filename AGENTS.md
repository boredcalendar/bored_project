# Agent Notes

"Sometimes you've just got to be bored, kid!"

## Product Intent

Bored Calendar is a playful digital wellbeing app. Its purpose is not to eliminate boredom or maximize productivity. Its purpose is to help people reclaim boredom as a healthy mental space.

Modern apps often train people to fill every quiet moment with feeds, notifications, tasks, or entertainment. Bored Calendar should be an antidote to that pressure. It should give users permission to be under-stimulated on purpose, notice the impulse to stay busy, and build comfort with empty moments.

The core benefit is space for reflection, creativity, emotional processing, and intentional choice. A good product promise is: appriciate your rare moments of boredom for what they are.

## Product Direction

- Keep the experience simple, playful, and emotionally approachable.
- Treat boredom as something worth practicing, not as a failure state.
- Prefer gentle prompts, tiny rituals, and lightweight reflection over heavy dashboards.
- Think of Focus Friends by Hank Green as a rough market-fit reference: small, friendly, health-adjacent, and feasible for a small team.
- Avoid enterprise productivity framing, guilt-based streaks, or clinical mental-health claims.
- Avoid features that require a large team to support unless there is a clear near-term reason.

## Useful Product Loops

- Log or schedule a bored moment.
- Sit with that moment instead of filling it immediately.
- Reflect briefly on what surfaced.
- See simple patterns over time without turning the app into analytics work.

## Monetization Direction

- Keep the core app usable for free with local-only data storage.
- Treat privacy-friendly local storage as a feature, not a limitation.
- Make paid cloud sync the clearest premium upgrade: backup, restore, and multi-device continuity.
- Avoid monetization that pressures daily usage, sells attention, or undermines the wellbeing promise.
- You can still manage your state manually like export/import between devices, but if you want to automate that you can pay.

## Current Prototype Context

This project started as a learning project by a beginner developer. Expect rough edges, unfinished UI, and old dependencies. Preserve the playful concept while improving quality incrementally.
